The module provides students with a basic understanding of statistical modelling and inference techniques used in cognitive science, machine learning and also in relevant professional fields in data analysis. They will understand the basics of statistical decision theory and statistical inference, and be able to identify and describe different approaches. Based on the understanding of statistical inference, hypothesis testing methods commonly taught in psychology will also be introduced. They know central methods, and can describe them mathematically and apply them independently. They will gain experience in understanding and using English terminology, as well as in the use of software for implementing statistical models.

As such, this course is different from many other introductory statistics courses for psychology or cognitive science. Some of the concepts taught in this class are fundamental in the Cognitive Science Bachelor as they explain how to make sense of data, which includes how a data scientists, a psychologist, and a machine learner should make sense of data, but also how the mind and the brain make sense of data.

Our goal is to strike a balance between nourishing intuitions, providing mathematical definitions and rigor where necessary, and providing you with programming tools, so that you apply these concepts and methods to your own projects. A particularly strong emphasis will be put on using simulations to develop intuitions about statistical facts and relationships.
